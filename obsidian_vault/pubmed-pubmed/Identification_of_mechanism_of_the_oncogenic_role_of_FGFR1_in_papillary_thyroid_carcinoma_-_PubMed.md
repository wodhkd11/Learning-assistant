# Identification of mechanism of the oncogenic role of FGFR1 in papillary thyroid carcinoma - PubMed

Papillary thyroid carcinoma (PTC) is the most prevalent malignancy of the thyroid. Fibroblast growth factor receptor 1 (FGFR1) is highly expressed in PTC and works as an oncogenic protein in this disease. USP7 stabilizes FGFR1 protein via deubiquitination, and YY1 promotes the transcription of FGFR1. FGFR1 downregulation impeded PTC cell growth, invasion, and migration and promoted apoptosis in vitro, as well as suppressed tumor growth in vivo. FGFR1 re-expression counteracted USP7 downregulation effects. The study identifies USP7 and YY1 as potent inducers of FGFR1 overexpression in PTC.
